Basics P1

What is a fraction?

A fraction shows part of a whole. The whole must be cut into equal parts.

⭐ The numerator (top) = how many parts you have. The denominator (bottom) = total equal parts.
Numerator & Denominator P1

Unit Fractions P2

Unit fractions — just one part

A unit fraction always has 1 on top. Examples: 1/2, 1/3, 1/4 ... 1/12

💡 The bigger the denominator, the smaller each piece! 1/8 is smaller than 1/4 because you cut into more pieces.

Comparing & Ordering Fractions
P2 P3
Like fractions P2

Comparing fractions with same denominator

When denominators are the same, just compare the numerators. Bigger numerator = bigger fraction.

3/7 vs 5/7 → same denominator! → 5 > 3 → 5/7 > 3/7
Unit fractions P2

Bigger denominator = SMALLER piece!

When numerators are both 1 (unit fractions), the fraction with the bigger denominator is actually smaller.

🍕 Imagine cutting a pizza: 1/8 slice is smaller than 1/4 slice because you cut into more pieces!
1/3 vs 1/5 → 3 < 5 → so 1/3 > 1/5
(fewer cuts = bigger pieces)
Unlike fractions P3

Comparing unlike fractions

Find a common denominator, then compare numerators.

Compare 2/3 vs 3/4
Common denominator = 12
2/3 = 8/12    3/4 = 9/12
8/12 < 9/12 → 2/3 < 3/4
Closest to 1 P3

Which fraction is closest to 1? P3

Subtract each fraction from 1. The smallest gap = closest to 1.

⚠️ Saina got this wrong in WA1 Q3! She picked 5/4 (which is greater than 1) instead of finding the smallest gap below 1.
Which of 4/5, 6/7, 5/4, 7/6 is closest to 1?

4/5: gap = 1/5 = 0.20
6/7: gap = 1/7 ≈ 0.14 ← smallest!
5/4: this is > 1, so it's NOT closest to 1 from below
7/6: also > 1

Answer: 6/7 is closest to 1
🔑 Step 1: Remove any fractions greater than 1. Step 2: Subtract remaining from 1. Step 3: Smallest gap wins!
Number line P3

Fraction between two fractions

To find a fraction exactly between two fractions, find a common denominator, then find the middle.

Find X exactly between 1/5 and 1/3:
1/5 = 3/15,   1/3 = 5/15
Middle = 4/15
X = 4/15

Equivalent Fractions & Decimals
P3 P5
Equivalent fractions P3

Same value, different names

Equivalent fractions look different but have the same value.

🔑 The golden rule: Multiply (or divide) both top AND bottom by the same number. Never just one!
1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6 = 4/8 = 5/10

How? 1×2/2×2 = 2/4    1×3/2×3 = 3/6    etc.
❌ WRONG: 1/2 → 2/2 or 1/3 — you can't just add to the top or bottom!
Simplifying P3

Simplest form — divide by HCF

Find the Highest Common Factor (HCF) of top and bottom, then divide both.

Simplify 12/18:
HCF of 12 and 18 = 6
12÷6 = 2    18÷6 = 3
12/18 = 2/3
Fractions as decimals P5

Converting fraction → decimal

Divide numerator by denominator. Use long division!

7/8:   7 ÷ 8 = 0.875

3 1/5:   1÷5 = 0.2, so 3 + 0.2 = 3.2

5/8 (to 2 d.p.): 5 ÷ 8 = 0.625 → round to 0.63
💡 Common ones to memorise: 1/4=0.25, 1/2=0.5, 3/4=0.75, 1/5=0.2, 1/8=0.125
📌 From WA1: Saina correctly got 3/7 ≈ 0.43 using long division. From Ai Tong (2024): Express 7/8 as decimal. From Catholic High: Express 5/8 to 2 d.p.

Adding & Subtracting Fractions
P2 P3 P4
Like fractions P2

Same denominator — easy!

Just add or subtract the numerators. Keep the denominator the same.

2/7 + 3/7 = 5/7    (just add tops)
5/9 − 2/9 = 3/9 = 1/3    (subtract tops, then simplify)
Related fractions P3

One denominator divides the other

Convert the smaller-denominator fraction to the larger denominator, then add/subtract.

1/3 + 1/6:
1/3 = 2/6 (multiply top & bottom by 2)
2/6 + 1/6 = 3/6 = 1/2
Unlike fractions P4

Different denominators — find LCM!

Find the Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) of both denominators. Convert both fractions, then add/subtract.

2/3 + 1/4:
LCM of 3 and 4 = 12
2/3 = 8/12    1/4 = 3/12
8/12 + 3/12 = 11/12
⚠️ NEVER add denominators! 2/3 + 1/4 ≠ 3/7
Subtract from 1 P4

Subtracting from 1 whole

This is the most important skill for the word problems in Chapter 8!

1 − 3/7 = 7/7 − 3/7 = 4/7

1 − 2/5 − 1/4:
= 20/20 − 8/20 − 5/20 = 7/20
⭐ "The remaining" always = 1 − (what was used)
Missing fraction P4

Find the missing fraction

2/3 − ___ = 1/42

___ = 2/3 − 1/42
LCM of 3 and 42 = 42
2/3 = 28/42
___ = 28/42 − 1/42 = 27/42 = 9/14

Mixed Numbers, Improper Fractions & Division
P4 P5
Mixed numbers P4

What is a mixed number?

A mixed number has a whole part and a fraction part: 2 3/4 means "2 wholes and 3/4 more".

1 whole
1 whole
3/4
💡 2 3/4 = 2 complete circles + 3 out of 4 pieces
Improper fractions P4

Improper fraction → Mixed number

An improper fraction has a numerator bigger than denominator (e.g. 11/4). Convert by dividing!

11/4 → 11 ÷ 4 = 2 remainder 3 → 2 3/4

Mixed → improper: 2 3/4 → (2×4)+3 = 11 → 11/4
Fraction of a set P4

Fraction of a quantity

"Of" means multiply. Find the part by multiplying fraction × total.

2/5 of 20 pencils = 2/5 × 20 = 8 pencils

There are 40 students. 2/5 walk to school.
Walk = 2/5 × 40 = 16 students
Do NOT walk = 40 − 16 = 24 students
💡 From Nan Hua Primary 2024 WA!
Division as fraction P5

Division gives a fraction! P5

When you divide and the result isn't a whole number, express it as a fraction.

3 ÷ 8 = 3/8 (e.g. 3 litres into 8 cups = 3/8 litre each)

15 ÷ 6 = 15/6 = 5/2 = 2 1/2 m
(e.g. 15 m rope into 6 pieces = 2 1/2 m each)

5 cakes ÷ 6 boys = 5/6 of a cake each
⚠️ WA1 Q10: Saina converted 3 litres to ml and got 375 ml — but the question asked for the answer as a fraction in litres. 3 ÷ 8 = 3/8 ℓ!
