Basics

What is a Triangle?

A triangle is a closed figure with 3 straight sides and 3 angles.

💡 Every triangle has exactly 3 vertices, 3 sides, and 3 angles.
Key Rule

Angle Sum Property

The three angles inside any triangle always add up to 180°.

⭐ ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°
By Sides

Types of Triangles (by Sides)

EquilateralAll 3 sides equal. All angles = 60°.
Isosceles2 sides equal. 2 base angles equal.
ScaleneAll 3 sides different.
By Angles

Types (by Angles)

AcuteAll < 90°
Right-angledOne = 90°
ObtuseOne > 90°
Method

Finding an Unknown Angle

📝 Unknown = 180° − (sum of the other two)
Example 1

Find x: angles 50° and 65°

x = 180° − 50° − 65° = 65°
Example 2

Isosceles: top = 40°, find base angles y

40° + 2y = 180° → y = 70°
Example 3

Right triangle: 90° and 35°, find z

z = 180° − 90° − 35° = 55°
💡 In a right triangle, the other two angles add to 90°.

Core

Area of a Triangle

A triangle is exactly half of a rectangle when its base and height match the rectangle.

Area of a triangle

Area = 12 × base × height
💡 Height must be perpendicular (90°) to the base.

Critical Skill

🎯 Identifying Base & Height

Base can be any side. Height = perpendicular distance from opposite vertex.

⭐ Look for the ⊥ symbol (small square) showing 90°!

Practice

AD is the height. What is the base?

✅ Base = BC, because AD ⊥ BC.
Example 1 — Distractor Alert!

Not every number is base or height!

⚠️ 14 & 20 cm are slant sides — NOT the height! Only 12 cm is perpendicular.
Area = ½ × 16 × 12 = 96 cm²
Example 2

Right-angled triangle

⭐ Two legs = base & height. Ignore hypotenuse!

Legs: 10 & 13 cm. Hypotenuse: 17 cm.

Area = ½ × 10 × 13 = 65 cm²
Example 3 — Reverse

Area = 36 cm², base = 9 cm. Find height.

36 = ½ × 9 × h
h = (2 × 36) ÷ 9 = 8 cm
🔑 h = (2 × Area) ÷ base
💡 Simplify early! ½ × 10 × 15 → 5 × 15 = 75
Grid Method

🔲 Triangles on Square Grids

Count grid squares for base and height, then use the formula.

Base = 6 cm | Height = 5 cm | Area = ½ × 6 × 5 = 15 cm²

Fraction of Figure

12 squares (1 cm² each). Triangle: base 4, height 3.

Triangle = ½ × 4 × 3 = 6 cm²
Fraction = 6 ÷ 12 = ½
Two Strategies

🧩 Composite Figures

➕ Add: Break into shapes, add areas.
➖ Subtract: Rectangle area minus unwanted parts.
Example — Add

Rectangle (12×6) + Triangle (base 12, h 9)

72 + 54 = 126 cm²
Example — Shaded Part

Rectangle 14×8. Triangle DEC inside.

Base DC = 14, height = 8
Area = ½ × 14 × 8 = 56 cm²
💡 Where E sits on AB doesn’t change the area!
Example — Square + Triangles

Square (8×8) + two triangles (½×4×8 each)

64 + 16 + 16 = 96 cm²
Advanced

👁️ Common Base / Common Height

Same base + same height = equal area, even if the triangles look different!

💡 Both triangles: same base, same height → equal area!
Diagonals of a Rectangle

20×10 rectangle. C = 40 cm². Find B.

B + C = ½ × 200 = 100
B = 100 − 40 = 60 cm²
⭐ Opposite triangles = half the rectangle. Common exam question!
