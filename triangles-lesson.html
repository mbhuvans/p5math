Basics

What is a Triangle?

A triangle is a closed figure with 3 straight sides and 3 angles. It is one of the simplest shapes in geometry!

💡 Every triangle has exactly 3 vertices (corners), 3 sides, and 3 angles.

We name triangles using their vertices. For example, Triangle ABC has vertices A, B, and C.

Key Rule

Angle Sum Property

The three angles inside any triangle always add up to 180°. This is true for every triangle — big or small!

⭐ Angle A + Angle B + Angle C = 180°

Example: If two angles are 60° and 70°, the third angle = 180° − 60° − 70° = 50°.

Classify by Sides

Types of Triangles (by Sides)

Equilateral All 3 sides are equal.
All angles = 60°.
Isosceles 2 sides are equal.
2 base angles are equal.
Scalene All 3 sides are different.
All 3 angles are different.
Classify by Angles

Types of Triangles (by Angles)

Acute Triangle All 3 angles are less than 90°.
Right Triangle Has one angle = exactly 90°.
Obtuse Triangle Has one angle greater than 90°.
🤔 Saina, can a triangle have two right angles? No! Because 90° + 90° = 180°, leaving 0° for the third angle — impossible!
Method

Finding an Unknown Angle

Since all three angles add up to 180°, if you know two angles, you can always find the third!

📝 Unknown angle = 180° − (sum of the other two angles)
Worked Example 1

Find angle x.

Solution:

x = 180° − 50° − 65°
x = 180° − 115°
x = 65°

Worked Example 2

Isosceles triangle — find angle y.

Tip: The two base angles of an isosceles triangle are equal.

40° + y + y = 180°
2y = 180° − 40° = 140°
y = 140° ÷ 2
y = 70°

Worked Example 3

Right-angled triangle — find angle z.

z + 90° + 35° = 180°
z = 180° − 90° − 35°
z = 55°

💡 In a right-angled triangle, the two other angles always add up to 90°.
