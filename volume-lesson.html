Core Fact

📏 The 3 Units of Volume

1 mℓ = 1 cm³
1 ℓ = 1000 mℓ = 1000 cm³
mℓ/cm³ → ℓ : divide by 1000  |  ℓ → mℓ/cm³ : multiply by 1000
⭐ 1 cm³ = 1 mℓ — a cube of side 1 cm holds exactly 1 mℓ of water. This is the key bridge at P5 between liquid volume and solid volume!
⚠️ P2 uses only litres. Millilitres appear at P3. cm³ is brand-new at P5 — don't confuse it with cm² (area)!
P2 — Litres only

Reading and Comparing Volume in Litres

📌 At P2 we only use litres (ℓ). We measure, compare, and do 1-step word problems using ℓ.

Example: Tank A holds 5 ℓ. Tank B holds 3 ℓ. How much more does Tank A hold?
5 − 3 = 2 ℓ

P3 — Litres & Millilitres

Compound Units

📌 1 ℓ = 1000 mℓ. Compound form: 2 ℓ 350 mℓ = 2350 mℓ.
⚠️ When adding compound units, convert to mℓ first to avoid mistakes!

P5 — new concept
Formula

📐 Volume of a Cuboid & Cube

V = length × breadth × height
OR   V = base area × height
Cube: V = side × side × side = s³
📌 The unit is always cm³ or m³. Never write cm² — that is area, not volume!
⚠️ When the question says "height is twice its breadth", find breadth first, then calculate height before multiplying.

Two Types

🔍 Finding a Missing Dimension

📌 Type A — Given volume + 2 dimensions:
Missing dim = V ÷ (known dim 1 × known dim 2)
or: Missing dim = V ÷ base area
📌 Type B — Given fraction-full + liquid volume:
Step 1: Full capacity = liquid volume ÷ fraction
Step 2: Missing dim = capacity ÷ (other dim 1 × other dim 2)
⚠️ Always convert ℓ → cm³ (×1000) before dividing dimensions!
P5
Type A Examples

Given Volume + 2 Dimensions

Cuboid volume 81 cm³, square base of side 3 cm. Find height.

1
Base area = 3 × 3 = 9 cm²
✓
Height = 81 ÷ 9 = 9 cm

Volume 480 cm³, base area 60 cm². Find height.

✓
Height = 480 ÷ 60 = 8 cm
Type B Examples

Given Fraction-Full + Liquid Volume

A 100 cm × 30 cm tank contained 360 ℓ and is ⁴⁄₅ full. Find the height.

1
360 ℓ = 360 000 cm³
2
Capacity = 360 000 ÷ (4/5) = 450 000 cm³
✓
Height = 450 000 ÷ (100 × 30) = 150 cm

Empty tank 20 cm tall. 9000 cm³ fills it to ¾. Find base area.

1
Capacity = 9000 ÷ (3/4) = 12 000 cm³
✓
Base area = 12 000 ÷ 20 = 600 cm²

Core Idea

💧 Volume of Liquid in a Tank

Volume of liquid = l × b × depth of water
If fraction-full: Volume = fraction × (l × b × h)
📌 How much more to fill? = Capacity − current volume
💡 Depth of water ≠ height of tank (unless completely full). Always use the water depth in the formula, not the tank height!
⚠️ Remember to convert your final answer to the units asked — sometimes ℓ, sometimes cm³.

Two Strategies

🚰 Pouring & Filling

📌 Pouring between containers:
Water moved = initial volume − final volume (of the source tank)
OR = capacity of receiving tank − water already in it
📌 Filling bottles from a tank:
Water removed = initial volume − final volume
Capacity of each bottle = water removed ÷ number of bottles
⚠️ Always find both the INITIAL and FINAL volume of the source tank using the l × b × depth formula.

P5 Standard
Key Formula

🚰 Tap Flow Problems

Volume added/removed = rate (ℓ/min) × time (min) × 1000
📌 Convert ℓ/min → cm³/min first (×1000), then add or subtract from current volume.

Time to fill/empty: Time = volume remaining ÷ rate (in cm³/min)
⚠️ Overflow check: if water added + current water exceeds capacity → overflow = total − capacity. Water CANNOT go above the brim.

Key Principle

🪙 Objects Submerged in a Tank

📌 When an object is placed into water, it displaces water upward.

New height = (water volume + object volume) ÷ base area of tank
✅ Think of it as: everything occupying space in the tank (water + objects) pushes up to the same height. New height = total volume ÷ base area.
⚠️ This only works if objects are fully submerged and water does not overflow.
📌 Cubes placed in, then water added until fraction-full:
Water poured in = target fill volume − volume of cubes
